## 1. Client ask (verbatim)
> The board is really excited — they want the AI visit-summary turned on for every patient,
> automatically, as our headline feature. Let's make it happen this week. We already share data with
> the AI vendor, and we have a de-identified export for analytics, so the privacy stuff is handled.

## 2. What it really is

  vendor on standard SaaS ToS with **no BAA** (D13); (2) the "de-identified" export strips only
  `name` — **17 of 18 Safe-Harbor identifiers remain**, so it's re-identifiable (D14). This blocks
  the flip-it-on ask until closed.

## 5. Acceptance criteria (done-definition)
- [ ] Scrub removes/obscures **all 18** Safe-Harbor identifiers — a test asserts each of the 18,
      not just `name`; adversarial test plants an identifier in a free-text/notes field.

- [ ] Data-flow diagram shows the vendor boundary and where the BAA is (or isn't).
- [ ] Memo states the concrete precondition list before the summary can be enabled for everyone.
- [ ] The "flip it on for everyone" ask is explicitly **blocked** in writing until preconditions met.
