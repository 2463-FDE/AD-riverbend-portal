## 1. Headline: one person, multiple charts (candidate matches)

Collapsing patient rows by normalized SSN **with corroborating demographics** finds **3 candidate identities behind 5 patient rows** — **2 candidate duplicate rows (40% candidate duplicate rate)**. These are matches queued for human review, not resolved identities: per ADR 0005, flag, never auto-merge.

Candidate duplicate cluster (SSN match `412-…`, every pair corroborated by name/DOB/address):

| patient id | name | dob | created via |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1042 | Maria Gonzalez | 1971-03-02 | self_service |
| 1330 | Maria Gonzales | 1971-03-02 | self_service |
| 1588 | M. Gonzalez | 1971-02-03 | self_service |

SSN alone is treated as a candidate signal, not an identity: rows sharing an SSN with conflicting name/DOB/address are flagged non-mergeable (conflict) — and groups that corroborate only through a bridge row are flagged ambiguous — and both are excluded from these counts (none of either in this export).

Root cause: `services/intake-service/intake.yaml` sets `self_service_intake: true` with `match_key: none` — every self-service registration creates a fresh chart, with no de-dup on name, DOB, or SSN.

## 3. Match-key analysis: what would have caught this

| match key | candidate identities | candidate duplicate rows caught |
|---|---|---|
| `none` | 5 | 0 |
| `name_dob` | 5 | 0 |
| `ssn` | 3 | 2 |

`name_dob` exact matching catches **zero** of the candidate duplicates here: spelling drift (Gonzalez/Gonzales), an initialized first name (M. Gonzalez), and a transposed DOB (1971-03-02 vs 1971-02-03) each defeat it. Only the SSN survives all three data-entry variations — which is why ADR 0005 anchors the proposed match key on SSN, *corroborated by demographics* rather than standing alone.

## 4. Retrieval scores over the contractor's gold-set (the foil)

Retriever: **local embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2, cached, cosine top-k)**, top-k = 1.

| query | expected records | retrieved | recall | precision |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| show me Maria Gonzalez's allergies | [1] | [1] | 1.00 | 1.00 |
| what medications is James O'Brien on? | [4] | [4] | 1.00 | 1.00 |
| latest lab results for M. Gonzalez | [3] | [1] | 0.00 | 0.00 |

**Macro recall 0.67 · macro precision 0.67.**

These scores measure agreement with a gold-set that was written per *chart*, not per *human* — so they mislead in both directions. A **hit** can be harmful: case 1 expects chart 1042 and the answer "No known allergies on file" while the same person's penicillin allergy sits under chart 1330 — retrieval scores perfectly by citing the allergy-blind chart. A **miss** can be the retriever doing the right thing: the gold-set lists M. Gonzalez (1588) as a separate patient, so any answer that associates her records across her own charts is scored as a failure. **Passing this gold-set does not mean the clinician sees a complete record — it means the retriever faithfully reproduces the fragmentation.**

## 5. Conclusion

Retrieval quality is not the problem; record identity is. Any retrieval helper built on these charts inherits the fragmentation and lends it authority. Fix intake first: see `adr/0005-mpi-match-key.md` for the proposed MPI/match-key approach.
